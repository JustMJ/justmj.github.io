================================================================================
MULTILEVEL MODELING (MLM) TUTORIAL SUITE - CREATION SUMMARY
================================================================================

Five comprehensive Quarto (.qmd) tutorials created for a doctoral-level MLM 
seminar in OB/I-O Psychology at UGA. All files are located in:

/sessions/cool-confident-thompson/mnt/outputs/MLM_Tutorials/

================================================================================
FILE INVENTORY
================================================================================

1. week06_cross_level_interactions.qmd (427 lines)
   - Cross-Level Interactions: When group-level variables moderate L1→outcome relationships
   - Sections: Theory, data simulation with true interaction, lmer fitting, 
     simple slopes analysis, publication-quality plotting, effect sizes, pitfalls
   - Realistic scenario: Team climate moderates autonomy → job satisfaction effect
   - "Try It Yourself" exercises included

2. week07_model_comparison_fit.qmd (470 lines)
   - Model Comparison, Fit, and Estimation Methods
   - Sections: ML vs REML, likelihood ratio tests, AIC/BIC, R² measures, 
     step-by-step model building workflow, publication-ready results tables
   - Builds 5 nested models comparing null, random intercept, random slope, L2 
     predictor, and cross-level interaction specifications
   - Exercises on model selection, sensitivity analysis, competing hypotheses

3. week08_assumptions_diagnostics.qmd (503 lines)
   - Assumptions, Diagnostics, and Practical Issues
   - Sections: L1 and L2 residual diagnostics, influence diagnostics, Kenward-Roger 
     and Satterthwaite df corrections, heteroscedasticity detection, missing data 
     handling, robust standard errors, complete diagnostic workflow
   - Includes QQ plots, residual visualizations, Cook's distance analysis
   - Practical guidance on when assumptions matter and how to address violations

4. week09_power_analysis.qmd (453 lines)
   - Statistical Power for Multilevel Designs
   - Sections: Why L2 units matter more than L1 units, analytic power formulas 
     (Maas & Hox), simulation-based power with simr package, power curves, 
     design space exploration, study planning workflow, rules of thumb and limits
   - Effective sample size calculations, power for main effects vs cross-level 
     interactions, sensitivity analysis
   - Practical examples: designing adequate studies, comparing design trade-offs

5. week10_multilevel_mediation.qmd (492 lines)
   - Multilevel Mediation: Within and Between Effects
   - Sections: The problem with naive mediation on nested data, types of multilevel 
     mediation (1-1-1, 2-1-1, 2-2-1), decomposing within/between effects, 
     bootstrapping indirect effects, presenting results with path diagrams
   - Zhang, Zyphur & Preacher approach with step-by-step implementation
   - Complete realistic example: team climate → member safety → performance mediation

- Academic but accessible language (written for doctoral psychologists)
- Math kept intuitive (not overly formal)
