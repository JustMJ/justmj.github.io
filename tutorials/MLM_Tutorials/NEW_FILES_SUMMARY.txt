1. WEEK 11: Multilevel Moderation and Moderated Mediation (444 lines)
   ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   
   Data: 500 employees in 50 teams (realistic OB scenario)
   
   Sections:
   • Introduction: When indirect effects depend on a moderator
   • Review: Cross-level moderation + multilevel mediation (Week 6 & 10 recap)
   • Conditional Indirect Effects: Computing IE at different moderator levels
   • Simulating Moderated Mediation Data: autonomy → engagement → performance,
     moderated by team supportive leadership
   • Fitting Component Models: a-path and b-path models
   • Computing Conditional Indirect Effects: at ±1 SD from mean
   • Monte Carlo Confidence Intervals: Manually implemented in R
   • Index of Moderated Mediation: Rate of change in indirect effect
   • Presenting Results: Publication-ready table and figure
   • Try It Yourself: 4 exercises (dual moderation, moderated b-path, etc.)
   
   Key Output: Demonstrates how mediation mechanism strength varies with
   team leadership quality, with visualization and CIs.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2. WEEK 12: Longitudinal and Growth Curve Models (472 lines)
   ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
   
   Data: 100 employees measured at 5 time points (weekly well-being)
   
   Sections:
   • Introduction: Reconceptualizing time as Level 1 (observations in persons)
   • Unconditional Growth Model: Random intercepts and slopes over time
   • Simulating Longitudinal Data: Individual differences in trajectories
   • Fitting Linear Growth Models: lmer(outcome ~ time + (1 + time | person))
   • Interpreting Growth Parameters: Initial status, rate of change, variance
   • Adding Predictors of Change: Does intervention condition predict faster growth?
   • Nonlinear Growth: Polynomial and piecewise specifications
   • Time-Varying Covariates: Within-person effects that change per occasion
   • Plotting Individual Trajectories: Spaghetti plots with overlaid group average
   • Connection to Experience Sampling: ESM, diary studies, EMA
   • Try It Yourself: 4 exercises (moderation of growth, multiple outcomes, etc.)
   
   Key Output: Spaghetti plots showing individual growth heterogeneity,
   comparison of intervention vs. control trajectories.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3. WEEK 13: Three-Level Models and Complex Nesting Structures (473 lines)
   ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   
   Data: 500 employees in 50 teams in 10 organizations (pure hierarchy)
   
   Sections:
   • Introduction: When two levels aren't enough
   • The Three-Level Model: Equations and intuition
   • Simulating Three-Level Data: Clear data generation with all three levels
   • Fitting Three-Level Models: (1|org_id/team_id) syntax, interpretation
   • Variance Decomposition: ICCs at each level (team, org, within)
   • Adding Predictors at Each Level: L1, L2, L3 simultaneously
   • Cross-Classified Models: When nesting isn't pure (teams AND departments)
   • Simulating & Fitting Cross-Classified: (1|team_id) + (1|dept_id) syntax
   • Practical Considerations: Sample size, convergence, simplification
   • Try It Yourself: 4 exercises (cross-level interactions, variance decomposition)
   
   Key Output: Variance component tables showing ~10% org, ~20% team,
   ~70% individual variation; comparison of hierarchical vs. crossed models.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

4. WEEK 14: Bayesian Multilevel Modeling and Multilevel SEM (516 lines)
   ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   
   Data: 576 employees in 48 teams in 8 organizations (for Bayesian example)
   Also: 500 employees in 20 teams with multilevel factor data (for CFA)
   
   Sections:
   • Introduction: When Bayesian approaches help (small L2 samples, complexity)
   • Bayesian Estimation Core: Posterior = Likelihood × Prior (intuitive explanation)
   • Setting Up brms: Installation, syntax mirroring lmer
   • Fitting Bayesian Multilevel Model: Code with eval=FALSE note
   • Specifying Priors: Weakly informative vs. informative
   • Interpreting Bayesian Output: Credible intervals, Rhat, ESS, convergence
   • Model Comparison: LOO and WAIC criteria
   • When Bayesian Helps: Example with N_L2 = 5 teams (unstable freq estimates)
   • Multilevel CFA Introduction: Latent variables at two levels
   • Multilevel CFA with lavaan: Two-level factor structure demonstration
   • Try It Yourself: 4 exercises (prior sensitivity, posterior checks, CFA extension)
   
   Key Output: Bayesian posterior plots, comparison of freq vs. Bayes with
   small L2 sample, two-level factor model with measurement error.

5. WEEK 15: Course Synthesis, Reporting Standards, and Best Practices (565 lines)
   ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   
   Data: 400 employees in 40 teams (complete worked example)
   
   Sections:
   • Introduction: Integration of entire semester's concepts
   • Decision Framework: Flowchart guide for choosing MLM structure
   • Reporting Standards for MLM: APA-style methods checklist
     - Data and sample description
     - Model specification (structure, centering, estimation)
     - Results (ICC, fixed effects, random effects, model comparison)
     - Example properly-reported methods section
   • Common Mistakes in Published Research:
     1. Ignoring clustered data
     2. Inappropriate centering
     3. Insufficient L2 sample size
     4. Automatic interaction testing
     5. Ignoring moderation in mediation
   • Complete Worked Example (Question to Publication):
     - Research question: Initiative → Retention, mediated/moderated
     - Step 1: Check ICC and describe sample
     - Step 2: Fit main effects model
     - Step 3: Test cross-level interactions
     - Step 4: Visualize conditional effects
     - Step 5: Create publication table
     - Step 6: Report variance components
   • Open Science Practices:
     - Preregistration (OSF guidance)
     - Sharing code and data
     - Power analysis for multilevel designs
   • Where the Field Is Headed:
     - Multivariate multilevel models
     - Intensive longitudinal data (ESM, diaries)
     - Machine learning + MLM
     - Causal inference in multilevel settings
   • Recommended Resources: Textbooks, recent O/I-O applications, software
   • Final Reflection Exercises: 4 deep-thinking exercises
   
   Key Output: Publication-ready tables, conditional effects plots,
   model comparison statistics, comprehensive guidance for future studies.
