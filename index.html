What I Do

Broadly, the goal of my program of research is to investigate the drivers of team and multiteam system performance in complex environments such as the military, long-duration space exploration, and medicine. Substantively, I am interested in advanced quantitative methodologies (e.g., computational modeling, social network analysis) and how we can use them to better understand how people work together to solve complex problems.

Teams and Muliteam Systems

how do we effectively bring people together to solve complex problems?

Emergence and Team Cognition

How do collective properties develop from individual interactions?

Time and Processes

How can we integrate time into our theory and understanding to clarify the processes underlying psychological phenomena in groups?

Quantitative Research Methods

How can we utilize methodologies from other disciplines and advance current methodology to advance our understanding of teams?

R Resources

There is currently a scarcity of formal R training, and organizational researchers' are continuously adopting methods stemming from other disciplines making it difficult for individuals in the field of I-O psychology to learn quantitative methods and apply them using the R statistical language. To address this issue, I am devoted to developing materials that can be utilized to learn about advanced quantitative research methods (with a particular emphasis on the study of teams), and implementing them in the R statistical language. Materials will be listed on this site as they become available.
